## Economic Incentives Analysis

The American economy doesn't operate in a vacuum. It operates within a structure of incentives — some created by government, some by markets, some by social norms. Understanding those incentives is key to understanding why our policies work or fail.

---

## The Core Insight: Incentives Drive Behavior

The fundamental theorem of political economy: *people respond to incentives*.

This isn't ideology — it's observable reality. When you change the incentive structure, you change the behavior. Every. Single. Time.

Let's look at examples:

### Immigration

**The Supply-Side Frame:**
- Focus: How many people are coming? How to stop them?
- Policy: Border walls, raids, deportations

**The Incentive Frame:**
- Focus: Why are they being hired?
- Answer: Because someone makes a rational economic decision to hire cheapest available labor
- Solution: Change the economics so American labor becomes the rational choice

Which approach has actually worked? Neither yet — because we've only tried the supply-side frame. The incentive frame offers a fundamentally different path.

### Healthcare

**The Market Frame:**
- Promise: Competition will lower prices
- Reality: Consolidation has raised prices 20-40%

**The Incentive Frame:**
- Why do hospitals merge? Because it increases pricing power
- Why do insurers consolidate? Same reason
- The "competition" framework misses that consolidation *is* the profitable path

### Labor Unions

**The Rights Frame:**
- Focus: Protecting the right to organize
- Policy: Organize more workers

**The Incentive Frame:**
- Why do union membership rates keep falling?
- Because the incentive to organize — collective bargaining power — has been eroded
- The incentive structure has shifted away from labor, not the legal protections

## The Pattern

Every policy area in America follows this pattern:

1. A problem is identified
2. A solution is enacted
3. The solution addresses the symptom, not the cause
4. The underlying incentive structure produces the same outcome
5. Everyone laments that "policy didn't work"

The answer isn't different policies. It's *different analysis*.
