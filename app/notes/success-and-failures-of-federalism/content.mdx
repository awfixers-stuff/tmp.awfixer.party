## Success and Failures of Federalism

Federalism — the division of power between national and state governments — has been both a strength and a weakness of American governance.

---

## The Successes

- Laboratory of democracy: States can experiment
- Multiple points of response: Faster action in crises
- Proximity to citizens: Local accountability

---

## The Failures

- Race to the bottom: Competition can degrade standards
- Coordination problems: National challenges require national answers
- Gaps and inconsistency: Citizens receive uneven treatment

---

## What AWFixer Proposes

Federalism should be structured to maximize its benefits while limiting its costs — with clear delineation of when national action is necessary.
