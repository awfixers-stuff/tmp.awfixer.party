## The Woes of Interpositioning

Interposition — the doctrine that states can "interpose" between the federal government and citizens — has a troubled history in American constitutional thought.

---

## The Concept

Interposition posits that states can refuse to enforce federal laws they deem unconstitutional. In practice, this has been used to resist civil rights and perpetuate discrimination.

---

## The Problems

- Nullification in disguise: Undermines national authority
- Subjective judgment: Who decides what is constitutional?
- Weaponized states' rights: Often used to protect injustice
- Inconsistent application: Applied selectively when convenient

---

## What AWFixer Proposes

Clearer constitutional mechanisms rather than relying on interposition as a check on federal overreach.
