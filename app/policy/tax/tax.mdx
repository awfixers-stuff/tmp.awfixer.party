## Tax & Fiscal Policy

**PILLAR TWO: Pro-Business AND Pro-Revenue**

**The Problem:**
The debate over taxes has been trapped in a false binary for decades: *low taxes* (which favor business and the wealthy) vs. *high taxes* (which fund public services but are framed as anti-growth). AWFixer rejects this framing entirely.

**AWFixer Policy Positions:**

- **Broaden the tax base rather than raise rates.** A healthy, competitive economy with fewer monopolies, more small and mid-size businesses, and a larger middle class generates *more tax revenue at lower marginal rates* than an economy dominated by a small number of mega-corporations using sophisticated strategies to reduce their effective tax rate to near zero.

- **Close loopholes before debating rates.** Before any conversation about raising or lowering tax rates, the AWFixer Party believes the existing structure should be enforced and simplified. A flatter, cleaner, loophole-free tax code would raise more revenue at lower headline rates.

- **Tax investment in America's future, not income from labor.** The current system taxes wages more heavily than capital gains — meaning a nurse pays a higher effective rate than a hedge fund manager. AWFixer supports rebalancing this.

- **Support small business over corporate welfare.** Corporate subsidies, targeted tax breaks, and preferential treatment for large companies at the expense of small competitors distort markets and concentrate power. We support eliminating industry-specific tax carve-outs.
