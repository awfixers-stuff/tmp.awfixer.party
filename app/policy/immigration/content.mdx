## Immigration Policy

**The Core Insight**
The immigration debate in America has been almost entirely focused on the supply side — how many people are coming, how to stop them, how to find and remove those already here. This is the wrong frame entirely.

The more uncomfortable question: **Why are they being hired?**

Because someone is hiring them. Consistently. At scale. For decades. And that someone is making a business decision that American labor policy has made not only possible, but *economically rational.*

AWFixer believes that if you remove the economic incentive to hire foreign labor over American labor, you solve the immigration problem without militarizing the border, without mass deportation operations, and without tearing apart communities.

**AWFixer Immigration Positions:**

- **Fix the incentive structure.** Remove the economic incentive to hire foreign over domestic labor, and the behavior follows.

- **Employer accountability.** Meaningful penalties for hiring undocumented workers — scaled to employer size and profitability.

- **Close the outsourcing subsidy.** Eliminate tax policies that subsidize exporting American jobs.

- **Simplify legal pathways.** Fast, transparent process for businesses that genuinely need foreign labor.

- **Oppose mass deportation.** Logistically impossible, fiscally ruinous, and morally untenable.

- **Structured pathway.** For the 10–12 million undocumented already here: back taxes, registration, background checks, demonstrated community ties.
