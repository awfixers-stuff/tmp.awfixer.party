## Civil Standard of Living

### The Problem With Minimum Wage

The minimum wage debate is one of the most reliably unproductive arguments in American politics. One side picks a number and calls it justice. The other side picks the same number and calls it a job-killer. Neither side is entirely wrong, and neither side is solving the actual problem.

The minimum wage is a blunt instrument applied to a complex system. A dollar figure set in Washington means something very different in rural Mississippi than it does in San Francisco. It also measures only one dimension — hourly cash wages — while ignoring the full picture of what it actually costs to live a dignified human life in America today.

---

### The Civil Standard of Living (CSL)

Instead of mandating a minimum hourly wage, AWFixer proposes the development and maintenance of a **Civil Standard of Living Index** — a regularly updated, regionally adjusted measure of what it actually costs to meet a baseline of dignified existence in America.

**The CSL accounts for:**
- Housing (rent or mortgage at non-exploitative market rates for the region)
- Nutritious food
- Healthcare, including preventive care and mental health
- Transportation (including non-car options where relevant)
- Childcare and dependent care
- Utilities including broadband internet, which is now an employment prerequisite
- A modest discretionary component — because a life with no capacity for leisure, culture, or savings is not a dignified one

---

### Policy Mechanisms

Rather than mandating a dollar figure, the CSL creates a **publicly visible benchmark** against which compensation packages — wages plus benefits plus non-cash compensation — can be measured:

**Tax incentives for CSL-compliant employers:**
- Businesses that demonstrably compensate their full-time workforce at or above the regional CSL — counting wages, healthcare contributions, retirement matching, and other material benefits — receive favorable tax treatment
- The government isn't dictating pay; it's making fair pay the smarter financial decision

**Public procurement preferences:**
- Federal and state contracting would favor CSL-compliant employers
- If you want government business, you take care of the people doing the work

**Transparency requirements:**
- Large employers would be required to publish their median total compensation relative to the regional CSL
- Creates public accountability and reputational incentives without mandating specific dollar figures

**Regional calibration:**
- Because the CSL is indexed to actual cost of living by region, it avoids the bluntness of a single national number
- A small business in rural Alabama and a tech firm in Seattle are operating in genuinely different economic environments

**Regular updates:**
- The CSL would be recalculated annually by an independent economic body — not a politically appointed committee with an agenda, but career economists using standardized methodology
- This removes the political theater of minimum wage fights from the legislative calendar
