## Intellectual and Moral Honesty in Governance

**PILLAR THREE: Governance Reform**

---

### The AWFixer Core Argument

American political culture rewards confidence over correctness, soundbites over substance, and tribal loyalty over honest reckoning. Politicians advocate for policies they know to be ineffective because changing position is framed as weakness. The result is a governing class that optimizes for winning arguments rather than solving problems.

AWFixer believes:

- **Evidence-based policy, not ideology-based policy.** Every major policy proposal should include a clear statement of the problem being solved, the mechanism by which the proposed solution addresses it, and measurable outcomes. Policies that don't work should be revised or ended — regardless of which party proposed them.

- **Institutional term limits and rotation.** Career politicians develop incentives that diverge from those of their constituents over time. We support reasonable term limits for congressional leadership roles (not necessarily rank-and-file seats) and mandatory rotation off committee assignments.

- **Mandatory conflict-of-interest disclosure.** Any elected official or senior appointee who votes on, regulates, or oversees an industry in which they hold financial interests should be required to divest or publicly disclose those interests.

- **Honest language in legislation.** Bills should be named for what they do, not for what sounds good. A bill that loosens environmental oversight should not be called the "Clean Future Act." We support a nonpartisan legislative plain-language review office.

---

### Principles in Practice

**Term Limits:**
- Reasonable term limits for congressional leadership positions
- Mandatory rotation off committee assignments to prevent entrenchment
- Does not apply to rank-and-file seats

**Conflict of Interest:**
- Mandatory disclosure before any relevant vote
- Required divestiture or public disclosure
- Independent enforcement

**Evidence-Based Policy:**
- Every bill includes problem statement and mechanism
- Measurable outcomes tracked over time
- Failed policies revised or ended

**Legislative Honesty:**
- Plain-language bill titles
- Nonpartisan review office
- Misleading titles flagged publicly

---

### The Philosophical Consistency

Leadership requires the courage to be right even when it's unpopular — and the humility to admit when you're wrong. We hold ourselves to a higher standard of reasoning and ethical consistency.

AWFixer believes this is the foundation of trustworthy governance. Politicians who change positions based on evidence are not weak — they're honest. That's what we need more of.
