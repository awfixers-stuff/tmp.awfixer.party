## Foreign Policy — Principled Engagement Over Perpetual Intervention

**The AWFixer Foreign Policy Doctrine**

American foreign policy has, for the better part of three decades, operated on a default setting of intervention first, diplomacy second, and reflection rarely.

The results speak for themselves: trillions of dollars spent, thousands of American lives lost, and a Middle East no more stable than when we arrived. Meanwhile, relationships with major global powers have calcified into adversarial postures that serve weapons contractors more than they serve the American people.

AWFixer believes in a foreign policy that is **strategically clear, diplomatically active, and militarily restrained** — one that protects Americans everywhere they are, builds durable partnerships, and treats engagement as the first tool rather than the last resort.

**Key Positions:**

### PRINCIPLE ONE: Diplomacy Is Strength, Not Weakness
- Restore and fully staff the State Department
- Elevate diplomatic personnel to the same cultural status as military officers
- Require that military options be formally preceded by documented diplomatic exhaustion
- Invest in foreign language training and long-term regional specialists

### PRINCIPLE TWO: The Middle East — Strategic Restraint
- No military commitment unless threat to U.S. is **direct, proportionate, and unmistakable**
- Maintain strong bilateral relationships with Saudi Arabia and the UAE
- Protect American citizens and assets abroad with full force when directly threatened
- Support diplomatic resolution of conflicts

### PRINCIPLE THREE: Strengthening Key Partnerships
- **Saudi Arabia and UAE:** Deepen long-term strategic frameworks
- **Japan:** Treat as peer partner in Indo-Pacific alliance

### PRINCIPLE FOUR: Reintegrating Russia and China
- Sustained diplomatic engagement produces better outcomes than isolation
- Economic interdependence is a more durable moderating force than failed sanctions
- Multilateral frameworks require Russian and Chinese participation

### PRINCIPLE FIVE: North Korea
- Seize the rare leadership transition window for formal reintegration program
- Multi-phase approach: back-channel contact → economic integration → security framework

### PRINCIPLE SIX: The American Backyard
- America needs to get very serious about the issues going on in our backyard.
- for many years we have let issues in our backyard go unchecked, which has cost us American lives by proxy. it was not until the first Trump Administration that even an attempt was made to deal with issues going on in our backyard.
- We would like to clarify that one of the major driving factors of Crimes committed against illegal immigrants in our country has to do with exploitation of other illegal immigrants in our country and their counterparts in our backyard. AWFixer has taken notice of this and we will turn our attention on our backyard in a renewed mannor under AWFixer Political Party Administrations
- We do not plan on invading Mexico or Canada or other South American Countries, though we do plan on putting pressure on the narco-states and related cartels that do as much damage to their host countries as they do to ours to begin to repair this issue without having to resort to interventionism.
