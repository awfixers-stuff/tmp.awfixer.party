## Energy Policy — Nuclear Deregulation and the Honest Environmental Argument

**PILLAR SIX: Energy Policy**

---

### The AWFixer Energy Doctrine

America's energy debate has been trapped for decades in a false choice between economic growth and environmental responsibility. AWFixer rejects this framing entirely.

There is an energy source that is:
- **Carbon-free** in operation
- **Reliable** regardless of weather, season, or time of day
- **Scalable** to meet industrial, residential, and transportation demand simultaneously
- **Land-efficient** to a degree no other clean energy source approaches
- **Proven** over seven decades of operation across dozens of countries

That source is nuclear power. And America has been systematically dismantling its capacity to use it for fifty years.

This is not an accident. It is the result of regulatory capture, institutional inertia, fossil fuel industry influence, and a public fear campaign built on misunderstood science.

---

### The Historical Failure — Revisiting Project Independence

The story begins with Nixon's Project Independence in 1973 and the Nuclear Regulatory Commission that quietly strangled it.

The NRC's licensing process became one of the most expensive regulatory gauntlets. A nuclear plant that might take five years to build in France or South Korea takes fifteen to twenty years to license and build in the United States. Licensing applications run to millions of pages. Cost overruns driven by regulatory delay become arguments against nuclear's economic viability.

Meanwhile, the fossil fuel industry spent lavishly to ensure that natural gas faced no comparable regulatory burden. American nuclear capacity stagnated while France generates 70% of its electricity from nuclear and pays some of the lowest electricity prices in Europe.

### The Honest Environmental Argument

**Not all clean energy is created equal.**

**Solar Fields:** Utility-scale solar requires roughly **75 times the land area** of nuclear. Panels require mining (silicon, silver, indium, rare earths). 25-30 year lifespan creates a growing waste stream.

**Hydroelectric Dams:** Consistently destroy river ecosystems, fragment fish migration, alter downstream hydrology, and release methane in tropical climates.

**Nuclear — The Environmental Case For:**
- Land use: A nuclear plant producing 1,000 megawatts occupies roughly one square mile. The equivalent solar installation would require 75 square miles.
- Carbon emissions: Comparable to wind, 70x lower than natural gas, 100x lower than coal
- Waste: Remarkably small volume. All spent nuclear fuel ever produced fits in a football field stacked ten yards deep
- Wildlife: Exclusion zones often become de facto wildlife refuges

---

### The Safety Argument

**Statistical reality:** Nuclear power has the lowest deaths-per-unit-of-energy-produced of any power source in the world.

- Coal kills ~25 people per TWh (air pollution)
- Natural gas kills ~3 per TWh
- Wind kills ~0.04
- **Nuclear kills ~0.03**

Chernobyl was a catastrophic accident involving a reactor design Western nations never used. It killed fewer people than coal kills every week. Fukushima killed one person from radiation. The evacuations killed hundreds through stress and displacement.

The public fear of nuclear is not irrational — it is a response to vivid, dramatic events. But policy should be driven by evidence. Nuclear is extraordinarily safe.

### The AWFixer Nuclear Deregulation Program

**1. Overhaul the NRC Licensing Process**
- Hard timeline — final determination within three years
- Design certification rather than site-by-site review
- Streamlined appeal process
- Maintain rigorous safety standards

**2. Embrace Advanced and Small Modular Reactor Technology**
- **SMRs** — factory-manufactured, deployed at fraction of cost
- **Gen IV reactors** — passive safety systems, some can consume spent fuel
- **Fusion research** — sustained federal investment

**3. Phase Out Oil and Gas on a Defined Timeline**

AWFixer proposed timeline:
- **Years 1–5:** Expedite NRC reform; fast-track SMR approvals
- **Years 5–15:** Scale SMR deployment; replace gas-fired baseload with nuclear
- **Years 15–30:** Complete coal and oil phase-out; accelerate electrification

**4. Invest in a National Nuclear Workforce**
- Federal investment in nuclear engineering programs
- Apprenticeship and certification programs
- Competitive compensation

---

### Why This Is the Environmentally Serious Position

The measure of environmental policy is not how it feels — it is whether it results in less damage. On that measure, opposition to nuclear while opposing fossil fuels has contributed to policy gridlock that has benefited the fossil fuel industry.

Every year without new nuclear capacity is a year filled with natural gas. Germany's *Energiewende* resulted in burning more coal and paying higher prices while carbon emissions increased.

The planet doesn't care about ideological consistency. It cares about carbon emissions and habitat destruction. Nuclear is the most defensible large-scale energy choice.
