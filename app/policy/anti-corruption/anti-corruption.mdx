## Government Corruption — A Comprehensive Reckoning

**PILLAR NINE: Anti-Corruption**

### The AWFixer Core Argument

American anti-corruption discourse has a **compartmentalization problem.** When corruption is discussed in one branch, it stays there. Each institution argues its corruption is unique, a special circumstance, not as bad as it looks.

AWFixer rejects this entirely.

**Corruption is a power problem.** Wherever power concentrates without accountability, corruption follows. The American system was designed with this in mind. That architecture has been allowed to decay. We propose to rebuild it — comprehensively, without carve-outs.

---

### The Scale of the Problem

**Legislative Branch:**
- Members trade stocks while serving on committees overseeing those industries
- $200 fine for STOCK Act violations — essentially meaningless
- Revolving door between staff and lobbying industry
- Ethics committees staffed by the people they're supposed to oversee

**Executive Branch:**
- Regulatory agencies staffed by people from the industries they regulate
- **Regulatory capture** — the most consequential form of corruption
- Cabinet officials enriching former employers through favorable decisions

**Judicial Branch:**
- Limited financial disclosure for Supreme Court justices
- Undisclosed gifts and hospitality from parties with interests before the Court
- Self-administered recusal standards

**Military:**
- DoD has **never passed an audit** (decades)
- F-35 program: $400B+ and counting, no accountability
- Sexual assault cases prosecuted within chain of command
- Revolving door between Pentagon and defense contractors

---

### INSTITUTION ONE: Independent Anti-Corruption Commission (IACC)

**Structure:**
- 7 commissioners: 2 by President, 2 by each party congressional leadership, 2 by appellate judges, 1 by civil society
- Fixed 8-year non-renewable terms
- Removal only by supermajority + judicial review
- Budget constitutionally protected

**Jurisdiction:**
- All three branches including Supreme Court and military
- State/local officials involving federal funds
- Private contractors, lobbyists
- Former officials (no statute of limitations for concealed conduct)

**Powers:**
- Subpoena authority
- Independent prosecution authority
- Asset freezing
- Whistleblower protection
- Public reporting on all closed investigations

---

### INSTITUTION TWO: Ethics Reform by Branch

**Congress:**
- Mandatory stock divestiture into blind trusts
- Lifetime lobbying ban for members; 5-year ban for staff
- Real STOCK Act enforcement (penalties scaled to trade value)
- Independent Congressional Ethics Authority
- Earmark transparency on public ledger

**Executive Branch:**
- 5-year cooling-off period before industry employment
- Contractor disclosure of former official relationships
- Political appointee financial disclosure
- Biennial regulatory capture audit

**Judicial Branch:**
- Binding Supreme Court ethics code
- Mandatory recusal with independent review
- Full financial disclosure for all judges
- Judicial appointment transparency

---

### INSTITUTION THREE: Military Accountability Reform

**Financial Accountability:**
- Mandatory DoD audit within 4 years
- Criminal referral for falsified accounts
- Independent cost estimation for weapons programs
- 5-year cooling-off before defense contractor employment

**Military Justice Reform:**
- Complete removal of sexual assault prosecution from chain of command
- Independent prosecutor's office reporting to IACC
- Investigation authority over hazing and toxic command culture
- Contractor accountability overseas

---

### Why This Matters

Corruption addressed nowhere comprehensively means corruption everywhere. AWFixer proposes rebuilding the anti-corruption architecture across every branch, every level, and every uniform — without carve-outs, without exceptions.
