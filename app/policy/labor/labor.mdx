## Labor & Immigration — Fix the Incentives, Not the People

**PILLAR FIVE: Labor & Immigration**

---

### The AWFixer Core Insight

Most American political parties treat labor and immigration as separate issues requiring separate solutions. AWFixer recognizes them as **the same problem wearing different hats.**

The immigration debate has been focused on the supply side — how many people are coming, how to stop them. This avoids the uncomfortable question:

**Why are they being hired?**

Because someone is hiring them. Consistently. At scale. For decades. And that someone is making a business decision that American labor policy has made economically rational.

Fix the incentive structure. The behavior follows.

---

### PRINCIPLE ONE: Remove the Incentive to Hire Foreign Labor

**The Problem:**
American businesses hire undocumented workers and offshore American jobs for the same reason — it's cheaper. Not because American workers are unwilling, but because the system has been structured to make exploitable labor more accessible.

The enforcement model — raids, deportations, border walls — attacks the symptom while leaving the disease intact.

**AWFixer Policy Positions:**

- **Employer accountability over worker punishment.** Meaningful penalties scaled to employer size and profitability.

- **Close the outsourcing subsidy.** Eliminate tax deductions for moving operations overseas. American taxpayers effectively subsidize the export of American jobs.

- **Level the playing field.** Import standards requiring goods to meet baseline labor and environmental benchmarks.

- **Simplify legal work authorization.** Fast, transparent process for employers who genuinely need specialized foreign labor.

- **Pathways, not purges.** Structured pathway to legal status for 10–12 million undocumented — back taxes, registration, background checks. Not amnesty without conditions.

---

### PRINCIPLE TWO: Reforming Labor Unions

**The Honest Assessment:**

Labor unions were one of the most important institutions in developing the American middle class. The 40-hour work week, the weekend, workplace safety standards — these came from organized labor.

But unions today are a mixed picture. Some remain effective advocates. Many have become political fundraising machines with labor branding. Decades of lobbying Congress has produced a cozy interdependency that serves union leadership while delivering diminishing returns for members.

Corruption, leadership entrenchment, and prioritization of political influence over member outcomes have damaged credibility.

Union membership in the private sector has declined from 35% in the 1950s to under 6% today. It's intellectually dishonest to declare the current structure sound.

**AWFixer Policy Positions:**

- **Independent Labor Union Oversight Board (ILUOB).** Structured like an independent judiciary — nonpartisan process, fixed terms, conflict-of-interest requirements. Audit union finances, investigate corruption, ensure democratic governance.

- **Separate union political activity from union labor activity.** Unions should be effective at negotiating wages. Political donations should be strictly separated and require explicit member opt-in.

- **Restore and modernize organizing rights.** Stronger penalties for illegal retaliation. We're not anti-union — we're pro-unions that are genuinely accountable.

- **Extend oversight to public sector unions.** The conflict of interest in public sector negotiations — where unions bargain against officials whose campaigns they funded — is structural.

### PRINCIPLE THREE: Civil Standard of Living Over Minimum Wage

**The Problem With Minimum Wage:**

The minimum wage debate is one of the most reliably unproductive arguments in American politics. One side picks a number and calls it justice. The other calls it a job-killer. Neither solves the actual problem.

A dollar figure set in Washington means something very different in rural Mississippi than in San Francisco. It measures only one dimension — hourly cash wages — while ignoring what it actually costs to live a dignified life.

**The AWFixer Alternative: Civil Standard of Living (CSL)**

A regularly updated, regionally adjusted measure of what dignified existence actually requires:

- Housing (rent at non-exploitative rates)
- Nutritious food
- Healthcare
- Transportation
- Childcare
- Utilities (including broadband)
- Modest discretionary component

**Why This Works Better:**

Rather than mandating a dollar figure, the CSL creates a **publicly visible benchmark** against which compensation can be measured:

- **Tax incentives** for CSL-compliant employers
- **Public procurement** preferences for CSL-compliant businesses
- **Transparency requirements** — large employers publish median compensation relative to CSL
- **Regional calibration** — acknowledges economic reality
- **Regular updates** by independent economic body

Minimum wage sets a floor and dares businesses to stay above it. The CSL makes meeting it the path of least resistance.
