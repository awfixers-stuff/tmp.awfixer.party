## Technology & AI Policy

### The AWFixer Position

Technology should serve the American people, not control them. AWFixer believes in a framework that promotes innovation while ensuring accountability, transparency, and protection of civil liberties.

---

### Government AI Requirements

**Open Source Mandate:**
- Any AI system used by any governmental body must be open source
- All government AI models must be audited by independent third parties
- AI decisions affecting citizens must be explainable
- Citizens must have the ability to contest AI-made decisions

**Transparency Requirements:**
- Publication of all AI training data sources
- Disclosure of model capabilities and limitations
- Regular bias audits by independent researchers

---

### Private AI Accountability

**Tiered Accountability Framework:**
AI systems that make consequential decisions about people's lives face tiered accountability requirements scaled to their impact:

- **High-impact decisions** (employment, credit, healthcare): Full audit requirements, explainability mandates, contestability mechanisms
- **Medium-impact decisions** (recommendations, pricing): Disclosure requirements, appeal mechanisms
- **Low-impact decisions** (entertainment, convenience): Light touch regulation

**Liability Standards:**
- AI developers liable for foreseeable harms
- Clear frameworks for determining responsibility in automated systems

---

### Computer Fraud and Abuse Act Reform

**The Current Problem:**

The Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) was written in 1986 and treats accidental access the same as intentional hacking. It has been used to prosecute security researchers, journalists, and whistleblowers who exposed legitimate vulnerabilities.

**AWFixer's Position:**

- Repeal the CFAA and replace with a framework that treats intent and harm — not system owner authorization — as the legal standard
- Good-faith security research is explicitly protected
- Curiosity is not a crime — it should be rewarded
- Vulnerability disclosure programs encouraged and protected

---

### Summary

| Issue | AWFixer Position |
|---|---|
| Government AI | Open source, audited, explainable, contestable |
| Private AI | Tiered accountability scaled to impact |
| CFAA | Repeal and replace with intent/harm-based framework |
| Security research | Explicitly protected, not criminalized |
