## Healthcare Policy

**The Problem:**
In no sector is the gap between free-market rhetoric and free-market reality wider than in American healthcare. Since the 1990s, hospital systems, insurance companies, and pharmaceutical distributors have undergone massive consolidation — all under regulatory frameworks that nominally protect competition.

**AWFixer Healthcare Positions:**

### Hospital Consolidation
- Hospital mergers have repeatedly been shown to *raise prices* in the markets they affect
- Studies have found price increases of 20–40% in markets with little remaining competition
- AWFixer supports aggressive antitrust enforcement in healthcare markets

### Insurance Consolidation
- Many regions have one or two carriers offering employer plans
- Reduces leverage workers have to demand better coverage
- Support competition-enabling regulatory frameworks

### Pharmaceutical Pricing
- The government is legally *prohibited* from negotiating Medicare drug prices in bulk
- This carve-out was written by legislators receiving pharmaceutical industry donations
- Support allowing Medicare price negotiation

### The Core Insight
This is the pre-WWI Gilded Age model in modern dress: essential services controlled by a small number of powerful actors, with political influence sufficient to prevent the kind of competition that would actually serve the public.
