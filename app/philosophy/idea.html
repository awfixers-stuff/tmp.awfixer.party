America does not have a people problem.
It has a structure problem.

Every policy AWFixer proposes begins from the same observation: when a system consistently produces bad outcomes across administrations, parties, and decades, the problem is the system — not the individuals operating inside it.

We do not believe Americans are uniquely corrupt, uniquely lazy, or uniquely incapable. We believe they have been handed a set of institutional incentive structures that reward bad behavior and punish good. We intend to change those structures.

Who We Are

The AWFixer Party is a new American political movement founded on the conviction that the country's most serious problems have been understood for decades — and that the reason they persist is not ignorance but institutional inertia protected by the people who benefit from it.

We are not a left-wing party. We are not a right-wing party. We hold positions that will satisfy neither side and challenge both, because we have organized ourselves around evidence and outcomes rather than ideology and tribe. If a policy works, we support it. If it doesn't, we say so — regardless of which party proposed it.

We believe the American political system has reached a point where its dysfunction is no longer a bug that can be patched. It requires structural repair. That is what this party exists to do.

"Corruption is not a Republican problem or a Democratic problem. It is a power problem. Wherever power concentrates without accountability, corruption follows. The American system was designed with this explicitly in mind. That architecture has been allowed to decay."

The Diagnosis: Thirty Years of Regression

Since Ronald Reagan left office in 1989, the United States has drifted back toward conditions that defined the pre-World War I Gilded Age — concentrated wealth, weakened labor, regulatory frameworks that serve industry rather than the public, and a political class that optimizes for its own continuity over the welfare of the people it governs.

This is not a partisan claim. The regression has occurred under both parties, across multiple administrations, through Democratic and Republican Congresses. It is the product of structural incentives that reward short-termism, punish political courage, and insulate incumbency from accountability.

The evidence is visible in every domain. Telecommunications deregulation that promised competition and produced monopoly. Financial deregulation that produced the Savings and Loan crisis — and then, twenty years later, the same mistake at ten times the scale. Healthcare consolidation that demonstrably raises prices while calling itself a market. An energy policy that forty years ago promised independence and delivered deepening dependence. Infrastructure that once led the world and now struggles to maintain what already exists.

The Pattern Is Always the Same

Bold public goal. Institutional machinery quietly built to prevent it. Decades pass. The goal is forgotten. The machinery remains. This is the cycle AWFixer exists to break.

The Nixon administration announced Project Independence in 1973 — full energy self-sufficiency by 1980. Then came the Nuclear Regulatory Commission, whose licensing process effectively strangled the nuclear expansion that independence required. America entered the 21st century more dependent on foreign energy than when the promise was made. Nobody was held accountable. The next administration made different promises and the same structural choices.

The Core Philosophy: Fix the Incentive

Every policy position AWFixer holds traces back to a single analytical question: what does the current incentive structure reward, and is that the outcome we want?

If businesses are penalized for hiring American workers relative to foreign labor, they will hire foreign labor. The answer is not to hunt down the workers — it is to change the penalty. If regulatory agencies are staffed by personnel whose careers are tied to the industries they regulate, those agencies will regulate in the industry's interest. The answer is not to fire individuals — it is to change the staffing structure. If congresspeople can trade stocks on information obtained through their committee work and face a $200 fine for late disclosure, some will do exactly that. The answer is mandatory divestiture, not moral exhortation.

This philosophy is neither liberal nor conservative. It is structural. It asks not "are people good or bad" but "what does the system make rational?" And it answers by changing the system.

What Makes AWFixer Different

Most political parties begin with a coalition and build a platform to serve it. AWFixer begins with a diagnosis and builds a coalition around the cure.

This means we hold positions that are genuinely uncomfortable for both sides of the existing political divide. We support nuclear power — which the environmental left opposes — on the grounds that it is the most honest large-scale clean energy answer currently available, and that the opposition to it while also opposing fossil fuels has benefited no one except the natural gas industry. We support aggressive antitrust enforcement — which the corporate right opposes — on the grounds that the free market requires actual competition to function and that what currently exists in telecom, healthcare, banking, and agriculture is not a free market.

We support real consequences for crime and a 77% recidivism rate as evidence of systemic failure simultaneously. We support open-source AI in government and the decriminalization of good-faith security research simultaneously. We support strengthening union organizing rights and establishing independent union oversight simultaneously. These are not contradictions. They are what it looks like to follow evidence rather than ideology.

Three in four people released from American prisons are rearrested within five years, at a cost of $40,000 per inmate per year. AWFixer treats this as what it is: a policy failure, not a human one. Real consequences and real rehabilitation are the same goal, not opposing ones.

On Bipartisanship

AWFixer is explicit that anti-corruption, anti-monopoly, and accountability frameworks must be applied without partisan selection. A Democratic senator trading on committee knowledge and a Republican contractor overbilling on a defense contract are the same problem. The same standards apply to a liberal judge who fails to disclose gifts and a conservative one. If AWFixer ever finds itself applying its principles selectively, it will have become exactly what it set out to dismantle.

The Platform in Brief

AWFixer's full platform is organized across ten policy pillars, each developed in detail on its own page. What follows is the thread that connects them.

Markets and Competition. We believe in free markets — which means we believe in competition, and competition requires antitrust enforcement as vigorously as it requires deregulation. The two are inseparable. Deregulation without competition is a monopoly subsidy.

Labor and Immigration. These are the same problem. The incentive to hire exploitable foreign labor over fairly compensated American labor is an economic calculation, not a moral failing. Change the calculation. Remove the offshoring tax subsidy. Level the import floor on labor standards. The behavior follows.

Energy. Nuclear power is the only large-scale clean energy source that is reliable, land-efficient, and proven at civilizational scale. The regulatory framework that has prevented its expansion since 1975 must be dismantled. Oil and gas must be phased out on a defined, honest timeline. Solar fields and hydroelectric dams carry real ecological costs that the environmental conversation has been unwilling to address honestly. AWFixer will.

Fiscal Transparency. Every federal dollar on a public, blockchain-style ledger. Permanently. Accessible to any citizen without a freedom of information request. The national security carve-out is narrow, independently reviewed, and subject to automatic declassification timelines. The Pentagon's failure to pass a single audit in its entire history is not a national security matter. It is an accountability failure being laundered as one.

Criminal Justice. Consequences are non-negotiable. Rehabilitation and consequences are not opposites. A Reform-Work Corps places eligible non-violent offenders on desert construction sites — nuclear plants, water infrastructure, transmission lines — where they earn wages, gain certified trade skills, and contribute to the national infrastructure agenda. The baseline they are competing against is a 77% recidivism rate at $40,000 per year. That bar is not high.

Anti-Corruption. An Independent Anti-Corruption Commission with jurisdiction across all three federal branches, all levels of government, and the military. Multi-partisan appointment. Supermajority removal only. Subpoena power. Independent prosecution authority. The revolving door, insider trading in Congress, undisclosed judicial gifts, defense contractor fraud, and military justice failures all fall under the same framework because they are all the same problem wearing different uniforms.

Technology and AI. Any AI system used by any governmental body must be open source, audited, explainable, and contestable. Private AI systems that make consequential decisions about people's lives face tiered accountability requirements scaled to their impact. The Computer Fraud and Abuse Act is repealed and replaced with a framework that treats intent and harm — not system owner authorization — as the legal standard. Curiosity is not a crime. It should be rewarded.

Foreign Policy. Diplomacy first. Military force reserved for direct, proportionate, unmistakable threats to Americans. Strategic relationships with Saudi Arabia, the UAE, and Japan deepened as genuine long-term partnerships. Russia and China reintegrated into multilateral frameworks rather than further alienated into an axis. A formal multi-phase North Korea engagement program that treats the coming leadership transition as the rare diplomatic window it is.

The Core Belief

Power Without Accountability Produces Predictable Outcomes

This is not a new observation. It is as old as political thought. The American system was designed around it. AWFixer exists to rebuild the architecture that was meant to prevent it.
