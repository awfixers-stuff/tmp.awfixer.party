## North Korea

# A History - Understanding the Issues:

One of the most critical things that must be understood about the Korean Peninsula is that the war between North and South Korea never formally ended. We, the Americans, and the UN arranged for a ceasefire and withdrew our own Troops and Peacekeepers. There was never a formal treaty signed. We believe that we have an upcoming rare opportunity to push for a formal treaty and begin the process of reintegration of North Korea with the rest of the world.

One thing that North Korea is known for doing is the hacking of banks and other financial outfits. We say that this is the equivalent of a hopeless american stealing food. North korea is in financial ruin, struggling to feed its citizens and has other issues that make their hacking and stealing something we think is more of a need than a wish of harm on the rest of the world.

We would also like to note the Nuclear Weapons program in North Korea. It has been noted in interviews that they believe the Nuclear Weapons have made them a country that is unlikely to be invaded and that they were not built because they want to invade other countries. This is a small but important details that AWFixer believes signals a way to work with North Korea in the coming years.

**The Problem:**
The coming leadership transition in North Korea presents a rare diplomatic window. AWFixer advocates for proactive engagement while maintaining strong deterrence.

**AWFixer Policy Positions:**

- **Engagement:** Pursue direct talks without preconditions
- **Denuclearization:** Incremental approach—relief for verified steps. AWFixer is open to Nuclear Power plants. Since North Korea has built nuclear missiles and only tested them, this signals that they will be a great steward of Nuclear Power Plants that will help them build a modern economy and reintegrate with the rest of the world's economy.
- **Alliances:** Coordinate with South Korea and Japan
- **Human Rights:** Maintain pressure on humanitarian issues separate from nuclear talks
