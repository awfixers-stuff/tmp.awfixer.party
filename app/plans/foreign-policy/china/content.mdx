## China

### A Background

China and America were allies in rhetoric in World War One, China assited with logistics and construction during the Germanic part of WWI. China was also an official American Allied Partner during World War Two and President Roosevelt called the Chinese Nationalist leader Chiang Kai-shek one of the "Four Policemen" in the major Allied powers, which signaled their entry into the UN Security Council in 1945 and gained them a Permanent seat they have retained to this day.

We believe that a relationship with China and the CCP can be rebuilt and will be a great part of the future of the Global Economy.

**The Problem:**
China represents both our greatest economic competitor and our most significant geopolitical challenge. AWFixer approaches US-China relations with clear eyes: competition where necessary, cooperation where possible.

**AWFixer Policy Positions:**

- **Decoupling:** Reduce critical supply chain dependencies in semiconductors, rare earth minerals, and pharmaceutical ingredients
  - AWFixer has been reading the history of American industry for a very long time, and has concluded that America does not need to be so dependent on non-american sources for anything. We have all of the materials we need to produce whatever we need and what we do not have we know the science of building ourselves.
- **Alliances:** Build Relationships of Genuine reciprocity with nations like Japan and Taiwan and work to reduce Chinese Aggression in the indo-pacific and reduce the need for alliances like NATOs precense in the region.
- **Trade:** Work to end tariffs from To and From China as we move industry back to America. An open flow of trade with China is to be considered
  - AWFixer is aware of the constant framing of China as a negative power and the cybersecurity risks that come out of Chinese goods. AWFixer would like to make you aware of the same issues that come our of cheap American, Korean and Japanese goods and ask you to consider if the quality of the product has anything to do with the security of it.
- **Technology:** Work with China on regulation of AI and containing their AI ambitions to their own Citizens rather than threatening other Powers like Russia and America, which only leads to cold war style tensions and make stabilizing the relationship harder.
- **Diplomatic Engagement:** Direct engagement with Beijing on climate, nuclear proliferation, and trade. No Cold War rhetoric—pragmatic competition is more effective than ideological confrontation.
- **Chinese Issues:** even though China is often framed in a negative light, China is currently struggling with one of the worse birthrate crisis in the world and AWFixer is more than willing to work with them on this issue.
